For Barry — the honest compliance picture
What native video does & doesn't do for HIPAA

Going native gives you the strongest privacy posture in telehealth. But the important truth up front: HIPAA follows the doctor, not the wire — it's about protecting patient information across the whole practice, not just the video.

What the native build already wins
✓

Encrypted by default

WebRTC scrambles the video and audio (DTLS‑SRTP) automatically, end to end.

✓

No third‑party vendor holds the visit

No Zoom or Jitsi in the path — so no vendor breach risk and no vendor agreement to depend on.

✓

Nothing stored

The encounter isn't recorded or saved on a server — there's no copy "at rest" to leak. That's a stronger posture than most commercial telehealth.

Three things to close before real U.S. patient data
1

The TURN fallback

When a straight connection can't punch through a strict firewall, the call relays through a TURN server (still encrypted — it can't read it, but it's in the path). Clean fix: self‑host TURN, or sign a BAA with the provider. Until then it runs STUN‑only — direct or bust.

2

Signaling metadata

The switchboard only passes connection info, not health data, and room names are hashed so it isn't "this patient met the doctor on this date." Keep it that way and it's essentially a conduit.

3

Everything HIPAA that isn't the video

Hub logins & access control, audit logs, encryption‑at‑rest for notes and messages, a BAA between Accelerated Experiences and you, patient consent to telehealth, and a risk analysis. Native video doesn't touch these — they're about the whole practice.

Which law even applies
🇻🇳 Your Vietnam patients

HIPAA is a U.S. statute — for patients treated in Vietnam it very likely doesn't apply at all. Vietnamese privacy & medical‑confidentiality law governs instead. This lane is live now.

🇺🇸 Any U.S. patients

HIPAA applies here. This is the lane the hub keeps gated until the three items above are closed (self‑hosted TURN + BAA + at‑rest encryption). Two paths, one system.

Messaging note: patient ⇆ provider messages are kept in demo / non‑PHI mode (a banner asks people not to share medical details yet) until message bodies are encrypted at rest or moved to a covered store. Video is already on the strong side; messaging waits for that one step.
The honest fine print: this page explains the posture in good faith — it is not legal advice, and nothing here claims a formal "HIPAA‑certified" stamp. Cross‑border care (U.S. HIPAA and Vietnamese law) is exactly where a healthcare‑privacy attorney should bless the final setup before real patient data flows.
